Bootstrap, anyone?

May 7th, 2014

As you can see I've discovered how to use Twitter Bootstrap. What an amazing tool. At first I felt a little cheap, like I was just copy/pasting a template. That's fundamentally what is happening, but there is actually a whole lot of HTML/CSS/JavaScript learning going on as you figure out how to reverse-engineer this monstrous codebase to do exactly what you want it to do. In addition to all that hands-on experience with refactoring/re-purposing code, you get a website that doesn't look like it was cutting edge in 1999. Bonus!

I'll continue to tweak the look of the blog but I'm pretty happy with it for right now. Unfortunately I was a bit short sighted and didn't remember to grab some screenshots of the previous design - my first ever attempt at modern HTML and CSS. That would have been fun to look at down the road.

I'm continuing to work on my CSTools project and have been steadily adding apps to it. I actually use the apps myself a fair amount throughout my workday. Though they're extremely basic, they do end up saving me quite a bit of time so I consider them successful at what they set out to do.

Next on my plate: I'm toying with a few in-browser implementations of Python to try and get my Text Adventure online for friends to check out. Skulpt was initially promising, but it's turning into a bit of a dead end for my purposes. I'll be looking into Brython next.

Sparse, but Functional

April 24th, 2014

Obviously design is not a strength of mine at the moment. This thing is very Geocities, but it works. Just learning how to get a website together with Python and Flask and getting it to actually be on the internet somewhere has been very educational. I'm using what I'm learning to cobble together a suite of extremely simple web apps to (hopefully) make a few things at work a bit easier or faster. I have two in mind right now: a template using HTML forms and Python to draft a boilerplate Outlook email, and a program to quickly tell if an institution's DEA license has expired. I'm sure I can think of more as I get deeper into Flask. I should probably also learn some jQuery/get back to JavaScript and figure out how to integrate that with Flask at some point to spice this gross little website up.

Blogging is not really my style but it's extremely helpful as far as practicing and learning more programming concepts/technologies. Sometimes I just feel like Creed Bratton.
