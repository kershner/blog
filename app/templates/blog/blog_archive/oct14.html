At Long Last

October 4th, 2014

Three months later, the GIF Display is finally complete!

Reading my old posts and project updates can be quite hilarious. I continually assume that the project will finish up "soon" or "next week", and as those estimates began to stretch further and further out I eventually just stopped mentioning it all together. Kerry was able to find the time to put the finishing touches on the absurd little device this week, and I absolutely love the results.

Kerry came across the primary image when flipping through a book of obscure curiosities she found while thrifting. It's entitled The Soul Hovering over the Body Reluctantly Parting with Life and was illustrated by William Blake for the re-release of the poem The Grave by Robert Blair.

She spray painted the bland Ikea frame and then decoupaged the illustration as well as the back matting. I love the texture brought out by the decoupage, as well as Kerry's subtle little touches like having the illustration slightly off-center relative to the frame and the LCD itself. I think it just looks great!

And there you have it. I'm not quite going to close the book on this one just yet, as I eventually want to get a larger screen which will involve Kerry re-designing her work, but that is a project for another time! I also still want to work on updating and finishing my write up of the process to be more useful and straightforward at some point. For now, be sure to check the GIF Display's project page and current write-ups.

On a related note, my repository of GIFs that I use for the display and GIF Party just hit 10,000 GIFs this week!
